Acidity of the Oceans in the Past Millenium

The ocean's pH has been dropping at a staggering rate in the past millenium. Animals aren't able to evolve at such a fast rate and the results are devestating.

What Actually is Ocean Acidification?

Ocean acidification is the process of the steady decline in the pH levels of the ocean due to uptake of CO2 by the ocean from the atmosphere over variable periods of time.

ANIMAL SHELLS

Increased acidity slows the growth of calcium carbonate structures, and under severe conditions, can dissolve structures faster than they form. If the acidity of seawater is beyond the optimum range for that organism, its body must use more energy to maintain healthy body fluid chemistry. Organisms can often compensate when faced with increased acidity, but this comes at the expense of using energy to grow critical body parts like muscle or shell. For example, scientists have found that mussels, sea urchins, and crabs start to dissolve their protective shells to counter elevated acidity in their body fluids. So even if an organism can adjust to survive increasing acidity its overall health can be impaired.

Raise Awareness and Education

Educate the public, policymakers, and businesses about the causes and consequences of ocean acidification.

Scientific Research

Invest in and support ongoing research to better understand the mechanisms and effects of ocean acidification. Encourage interdisciplinary research to study the impacts on marine life, ecosystems, and climate.

Reduce CO2 Emissions

Implement policies and regulations to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, particularly carbon dioxide. Promote renewable energy sources, energy efficiency, and carbon capture technologies.

Transition to Sustainable Fisheries

Implement and enforce sustainable fisheries management practices to reduce overfishing. Protect and restore critical marine habitats that support fisheries.

International Cooperation:

Collaborate on research, monitoring, and sharing best practices.



Regular Review and Adaptation

Continuously evaluate the effectiveness of implemented policies and adapt strategies as new information becomes available.
